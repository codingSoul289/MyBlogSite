October 16, 2021

 


                After what seemed like ages, here we are at the window of celebrations, a juxtaposition of contrasting themes, a phalanx of hand-made creative art forms of humanoid gods and goddesses, a period when almost every Bengali, irrespective of religion, caste or creed, eschews all relations with their daily and monotonous life cycle to embrace the occasion of Maa Durga descending from the heavens to visit her paternal abode - Yes, we are at the horizon of the 2021 Durga Puja vacations.

        In retrospect, just a few months ago, we were in the midst of the deadly and appalling second wave of COVID-19, the pandemic which roots back to the month of March, 2020, when it shook the entire world with its repercussions and long-lasting consequences. The first wave had dwindled down to around 9000-12000 cases per day in India, around the months of January and February, 2021. Every person living in the country saw some hope, a light at the end of the tunnel. It was at this point that the second wave had planted its ferocious seeds, and gnawed away at almost every household. Around a few million cases had been noted with many turning fatal, exhibiting a horrifying event in the history of the Indian civilisation.

        Though the second wave had started to dwindle down in the months of August and September, it didn't fully disappear. West Bengal itself had envisioned a spectacle she never wishes to be repeated again. We needed some relief, a period of forgetting the past, the present and the future, boycotting the concept of time itself, to breathe in some ounce of life. Ergo, this year's Durga Puja was a cause of replenishment more than any other year, it was an event that almost every Bengali wished to absorb forever in their veins and arteries, to learn to move on from the deadly times towards a brighter day ahead. Yes, the period from 6th October till 15th October (and even further for some) was a period of rebirth for us - to gain the energy and zeal for presenting ourselves at the helm of life again. 

        With such points in mind, here is a virtual exhibit of the major Durga Puja pandals along with a gist of their themes in Kolkata and Serampore -

KOLKATA PANDALS - 2021

        Here, I would like to take you on an enthralling journey through a vivid nexus of colours and lightings and decorations in the courtyard of the City of Joy this year.

a. Dum Dum Park Bharat Chakra Club

    Theme - Farmers' Protests


    Affixed to the theme of the ongoing farmers' protest to the inclusion of the 3 controversial farm laws in the Parliament, this pandal showcases the daily rigors in a farmer's life, their unheard cries and the sacrifices in the lives of the employees of one of the largest sectors in India. Adorned with chappals and sandals, this very image tells the tale of the multitude of farmers across India, specially those who had either lost their lives in the name of the protests or were left in a fatal condition by the so-called "Law and Order" system. The sandals represent the juxtaposition of the protest steps and their daily lives on the fields, leading to formation of specks (represented by the farmer faces) on their feet.

b. Barisha Club's "Bhager Maa", Behala

    Theme - Migrants in the midst of NRC/CAA (Bhager Maa)



       Singing out the rhythm and lyrics of the unheard and unseen plethora of "human beings" being partitioned in the "21st Century" in the name of the "narrow domestic walls" of religion and sects, this magnum opus of Barisha Club in Behala stands atop these attempts at clashing interests of so many innocents being drifted out from India. Yes, you guessed it right - this Club posited their theme based on the NRC/CAA incidents and the controversies surrounding them. Very wittily, the artists had divided the entire pandal into two major segments - on one side remains Bangladesh and on the other side is India, with an appalling spectacle of a dismayed mother (also referred to as "Bhager Maa" or the Mother in the midst of partition) in the centre with her equally horrified children, holding a sculpture of Maa Durga in her laps. This very image takes us to the forefront of horrors faced by ordinary people whose lives are this uncertain - one day, living to make ends meet on one side of the border, and the next day being discarded as living on this soil. Maa Durga here is also represented by one such mother, carrying children on her laps, reminding the people once more to not fall prey into the seeds of hatred planted in the modern society.

c. Prafulla Kanan Durga Puja Committee, Keshtopur - 

    Theme - Fishermen stranded by floods in Yash and help meted out by the actor and philanthropist, Sonu Sood


      Displaying the predicament faced by the fishermen along the coastlines of the Bay of Bengal due to the emergence of Yaas Supercyclone in May 2021, this pandal has brought our their lives in such a rigorous environment. Maa Durga along with her children - Karthik, Saraswati, Lakshmi and Ganesha are seen embarking on their journey to help out the thousands of fishermen and those living along the coastline at that time, including Weather experts and journalists and police. One remarkable mention here is the effigy of Sonu Sood, the philanthropist actor (who had also made immense presence in the COVID-19 second wave, by helping out hundreds of thousands suffering at the time) who is seen providing food and other things as relief to the stranded fishermen and other sufferers in the Yaas debacle episode. The art form therefore, rightly depicts the man of honour who took up the work of Maa Durga (as shown) herself to distribute help at that time and shows immense respect to the man.

d. Babubagan Sarbojanin Durgotsava Committee, Kolkata

    Theme - Library


       Filled with the chronicles of libraries in West Bengal, dating back to masterpieces of creativity of some of the greatest authors, poets, liberals, modernists, etc. this pandal pays tribute to the concept of libraries in general. With a mind-soothing view of the external pandal as well as the internal display of tomes and frescoes of Authors and Poets alike, the Babubagan Club will be a pleasant experience for any bibliophile and art aficionados. The internal setting of the Pandal is as peaceful and vast as any library is. As a whole, this view epitomizes the setting of a perfect book-reading environment.

e. Shreebhumi, North Kolkata

    Theme - Burj Khalifa


    Most famous and popular pandal of 2021 across social media, Shreebhumi has captured the attention of not only Bengalis but many others from India and even outer countries - displaying a vibrancy of colours through a colossal 145-feet tall Burj Khalifa themed pandal. Burj Khalifa, as most of us know, is the tallest building in the world (around a 800 m tall) known for its lighting shows and effects during sensational occasions as well as paying tribute to many. Though falling way short of the actual height of the building in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, this pandal has put viewers in awe of the mimic of the lighting shows originally portrayed there. Although, due to complaints from three pilots of "obstruction in viewing" of runway in the nearest Dum Dum Airport, the lighting was called off from Navami, the Pandal still remains one of the sensational spectacles of Durga Puja alongside the 52-feet tall idol at Deshopriyo Park, 2015.

SERAMPORE PANDALS - 2021

        It would not be an exaggeration if I say that Kolkata Pandals had always been the best and the lit up streets of Kolkata during Durga Puja imbue hearts with emotions, so much so that people no matter what, would never be willing to remain at home and continue with the daily life activities. However, it would be an understatement to look down upon the other grand visions around West Bengal. So, here is a journey into the streets of Serampore, my hometown, during Durga Puja 2021.


a. Aat er Polli, near Jhau Tala

    Theme - "Uttaram" / Fighting against COVID-19

       Capturing the theme of COVID-19 and the Lockdown imposed by the pandemic, leading to a myriad lives stranded and many children losing out their most glorious days and precious education in pursuit of Online Classes and Tests. Thus, this pandal poses as the theme of "Uttaram" (a period of uncertainty and difficulty) during COVID-19 and its repercussions alongside portraying the horrors imposed by the Pandemic itself. With a view of "red" and "maroon", this site truly deserves some attention to the details.

b. Mallik Para, near Serampore Flyover

    Theme - Nature and God's embodiment as Wildlife

      Depicting the beauty of Mother Nature and the incarnation of the Almighty in the form of Wildlife, this Pandal has been set as a grotto of intertwined steps, covered in red seamed cloth beside the Flyover. It has attracted many, due to its unique themes every year, and has been set as one of the best pandals in Serampore for its themes and creative portrayal of such. With some insight into this beautiful landscape, this pandal truly deserves an attention span of a considerable amount.

c. Manna Para, Chatra, Serampore

    Theme - Creativity of a child's mind and cartoon forms

       Wonderfully exhibiting a child's imaginative brain and the concept of cartoons (here, portrayed by Batul The Great and other Bengali popular cartoons), this Club fixates the theme around children in general. A child is the embodiment of joy, happiness, carefreeness and innocence. Here, Maa Durga is portrayed as blessing children and their imaginative and creative mindset.

d. RMS Math, Serampore

    Theme - Temple


       A display of bright lighting and colours, this Pandal portrays a kind of a Marble Temple and also the heritage sites of India. It emanates a sense of holiness in itself and displays the vibrancy of India and its diversity in terms of heritage and culture.


e. Netaji Mor, Pravash Nagar

    Theme - Temple Elephants (of South India)

      This Pandal from Netaji Mor, Pravash Nagar, displays a typical South Indian Temple, with elephants, as its theme. A pandal covered in bronze matte, it represents the guilded temples from Kerala in general. A colossal pandal in terms of width, exhibiting the South Indian culture and heritage, it deserves a wondrous stare into the glory.


f. Nobarun Sangha, Manicktala

    Theme - Gilded Temple made of village baskets

      This pandal provides a dazzling view of a gilded temple, made surprisingly with straw baskets of rural India. Even the idol of Maa Durga herself portrays a dazzling gilded form, pleasing to any viewer who visits it.

Some Honourable Mentions


      A splendid display of colours and vivid highlights of remarkable work and intricate details at Gorghori Ghat, Chatra.


   A creative pandal at Forward Club, Serampore.

    
Mitali Shanshad Pandal


A pandal made with strings and ropes and mud pots near Lakhsmi Ghat, Mahesh.

                    With these, I brought you to a divine world of vibrancy and colours in one of the grand festivals of West Bengal, India. A festival which is cultivated from the creativity of artists from around Kolkata and other places. The world stands still for any commoner of Bengal during this time and we offer ourselves a time to enjoy without any worry for the world. With the end of Durga Puja on the 15th of October, 2021 (Dashami), a Bengali counts the days till the next Durga Puja comes. 

                    Till then, let the cry of "Asche Bochor Abar Hobe" reverberate through the streets.
